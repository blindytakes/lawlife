Why I Started Keibler Law Group

CK
Chase Keibler
Founding Attorney
January 20, 2026
4 min read

I grew up in a blue-collar town outside Pittsburgh. My dad worked with his hands. My mom kept everything running. Nobody in my family had ever hired a lawyer, much less imagined their kid would become one. But when I got to law school, something clicked — I wanted to fight for people who couldn't fight for themselves.

After graduating and passing the bar in both South Carolina and North Carolina, I went to work at a large firm. I cut my teeth on complex business litigation, antitrust cases, and insurance defense. I learned how the system works from the inside — how insurance companies evaluate claims, how they decide which cases to fight and which to settle, and how they treat the people on the other end of those claims.

What I Saw From the Inside

Working on the defense side taught me things they don't teach in law school. I saw how insurers used delay tactics to wear down injured people. I watched adjusters lowball claims knowing most people wouldn't push back. I saw cases where the injured person clearly deserved more, but their attorney didn't have the resources or willingness to go to trial.

The system wasn't broken in some abstract way. It was broken in very specific, very fixable ways — and I kept thinking: if this were my family on the other side, I'd want something better.

"Too many law firms fail their clients. I wanted to build one that wouldn't."

What "Client-First" Actually Means

Every law firm says they're "client-focused." It's on every website and every billboard. But here's what it means to me, specifically:

Limited caseload. Most personal injury firms take on hundreds of cases and run them through a machine. We intentionally take fewer cases so every client gets direct access to me — not a paralegal they've never met. Your case is never just a file number.

Obsessive communication. The number one complaint people have about lawyers is that they can't get them on the phone. At Keibler Law Group, you pick how you want to communicate — phone, text, email, video call — and we actually respond. If something happens in your case, you'll hear about it from us before you have to ask.

No surprises on fees. We work on contingency. That means you pay nothing upfront and nothing out of pocket. If we don't win, you don't pay. Period. No hidden costs, no hourly billing, no surprise invoices.

Trial-ready, always. Insurance companies know which attorneys will actually go to trial and which ones will fold. We prepare every case as if it's going to a jury. That leverage changes the math on the other side and consistently leads to better outcomes for our clients.

Why South Carolina — and Why Now

After relocating to South Carolina, I fell in love with the community here. But I also saw a gap. Too many people in the Midlands, the Lowcountry, and across the Carolinas were being underserved by firms that treated them like a number. I wanted to build something different — a firm that combines big-firm litigation experience with small-firm personal attention.

That's Keibler Law Group. We handle catastrophic injuries, wrongful death, complex business disputes, and antitrust cases across South Carolina and North Carolina. We fight hard, we communicate obsessively, and we never, ever settle for less than what our clients deserve.

A personal note

If you're reading this and you've been injured or wronged, I want you to know: you don't have to figure this out alone. The consultation is free. The conversation is confidential. And if I can't help you, I'll tell you honestly and point you in the right direction. That's a promise.

Free consultation. No obligation. No fee unless we win. That's the Keibler way.
